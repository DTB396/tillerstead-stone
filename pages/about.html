Tillerstead LLC is what happens when someone actually reads TCNA standards instead of winging it. Licensed NJ tile contractor. Waterproof showers that pass flood tests. Documentation your insurance company doesn't laugh at. The boring stuff that prevents $12,000 mold remediation bills.

Why We're Annoying About Details

Tyler's the kind of contractor who measures substrate flatness with a level *and* a straightedge. Who knows the difference between modified and non-modified thinset (and why your marble installer better know too). Who's read ANSI A108.10 waterproofing specs and can explain why "some red paint" isn't a membrane system.

This makes us slower than "some guy." It makes us more expensive than the Craigslist quote. It also means your shower doesn't leak, your tile doesn't crack, and you're not Googling "mold remediation near me" in 18 months. Trade-offs.

Work includes occupied homes where we cover doorways and vacuum daily (revolutionary, we know), rental properties where timelines actually matter, and coordination with GCs who appreciate contractors who show up when scheduled and document everything.

What Working Together Actually Looks Like

You've been ghosted by contractors. You've gotten the "yeah I can do that" from someone who definitely cannot. You've lived through the "we'll be done Friday" that becomes "maybe next Thursday." Here's different:

  • Written everything: Scope, materials, timeline, payment terms, change-order process. In writing. Before work starts. Wild concept, right?
  • Daily photo logs: Substrate prep, waterproofing details, membrane corners, flood test results. So you know what's behind the tile before it's... behind the tile.
  • Texts returned same-day: Not "I'll call you back" that never happens. Not three-week reply gaps. Actual communication like professionals in 2024.
  • Realistic timelines: We say 2 weeks, it's 2 weeks. We don't say 3 days knowing it's 10. Shocking honesty.
  • Change orders in writing: Scope changes, prices adjust, you approve before work continues. No "surprise" invoices at closeout.

Is this revolutionary? No. Is it rare? Unfortunately.
