Tile Installation & Waterproofing (The Part That Matters Most)

Here's the thing about tile: it's not hard to stick to a wall. Kindergarten craft projects prove this daily. What's hard is building waterproof assemblies that survive South Jersey humidity, substrate movement, and thermal cycling. ANSI A118.10 waterproofing. TCNA-compliant substrate prep. ISO 13007 C2-rated thinset for large-format. The boring specs that prevent exciting insurance claims.

  • Waterproof showers that don't leak: Schluter KERDI, Hydroban, or RedGard membranes (not "red paint from the box store"). Installed to manufacturer specs. Flood tested before tile with photos you can show skeptical spouses. Proper slope (1/4" per foot), pre-pitch where required, and seam details done correctly the first time.
  • Large-format tile (24×48 and up): Substrate flatness verified (1/8" in 10'), not assumed. C2-rated modified thinset (because TCNA says so). Back-buttering on tiles >24". Lippage control systems on every install. The stuff that separates "installed" from "done right."
  • Backsplashes worth photographing: Outlet cutouts that don't look like someone sneezed. Clean terminations at cabinets. Caulked transitions (not grouted—wrong tool for the job). Layout that makes sense from where you actually stand, not what's easiest.
  • Floor tile that survives: Uncoupling membranes where deflection is a concern (wood subfloors, we're looking at you). Movement joints at perimeter and plane changes. Proper thinset coverage so you don't hear hollow sounds when walking. Grout sealed with penetrating sealer, not that glossy garbage that peels.
  • Heated floors that heat: Schluter DITRA-HEAT, SunTouch, or NuHeat mats with actual programmable thermostats (not the $12 dial kind). GFCI protection because electrical codes exist for reasons. Ohm testing before, during, and after to prove it works before we cover it in tile.
  • Shower niche ninja: Prefab foam niches (Schluter, Wedi) or site-built with proper waterproofing and slope. No mystery leaks five years later. No "why is there mold in the wall" phone calls.
